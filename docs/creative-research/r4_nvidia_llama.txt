Here are 10 creative research tools that combine web scraping, NLP, and graph analysis to gather intelligence using only free sources:

**1. Company Intelligence: "Company Insights"**
* Web scraping: Extract company data from Wikipedia, Crunchbase, and LinkedIn
* NLP: Analyze company descriptions, news articles, and financial reports using spaCy and NLTK
* Graph analysis: Visualize company relationships using NetworkX and Gephi
* Output: Company profiles, competitor analysis, and industry mapping (Free sources: Wikipedia, Crunchbase, LinkedIn)

**2. Hiring Signals: "Talent Tracker"**
* Web scraping: Extract job postings from company websites, Indeed, and Glassdoor
* NLP: Analyze job descriptions using Stanford CoreNLP and Gensim
* Graph analysis: Visualize hiring trends and skill requirements using Matplotlib and Plotly
* Output: Hiring signal alerts, skill demand analysis, and talent market intelligence (Free sources: Indeed, Glassdoor, company websites)

**3. Patent Landscaping: "Patent Pioneer"**
* Web scraping: Extract patent data from USPTO, EPO, and Google Patents
* NLP: Analyze patent abstracts and descriptions using TfidfVectorizer and scikit-learn
* Graph analysis: Visualize patent landscapes and citation networks using NetworkX and Cytoscape
* Output: Patent landscape analysis, innovation mapping, and competitor patent analysis (Free sources: USPTO, EPO, Google Patents)

**4. Regulatory Tracking: "RegWatch"**
* Web scraping: Extract regulatory data from government websites, RSS feeds, and news outlets
* NLP: Analyze regulatory text using spaCy and VADER sentiment analysis
* Graph analysis: Visualize regulatory relationships and impact using Gephi and Sigma.js
* Output: Regulatory alert system, compliance tracking, and policy analysis (Free sources: government websites, RSS feeds, news outlets)

**5. Academic Collaboration Mapping: "Research Radar"**
* Web scraping: Extract researcher data from academic profiles, ResearchGate, and Academia.edu
* NLP: Analyze researcher publications and interests using NLTK and Gensim
* Graph analysis: Visualize collaboration networks and research trends using NetworkX and Plotly
* Output: Researcher profiles, collaboration networks, and research topic analysis (Free sources: ResearchGate, Academia.edu, academic profiles)

**6. Market Research: "MarketMinder"**
* Web scraping: Extract market data from news articles, social media, and review websites
* NLP: Analyze market sentiment using VADER sentiment analysis and TextBlob
* Graph analysis: Visualize market trends and competitor analysis using Matplotlib and Seaborn
* Output: Market sentiment analysis, competitor analysis, and trend alerts (Free sources: news articles, social media, review websites)

**7. IP Analysis: "IP Insight"**
* Web scraping: Extract IP data from IP databases, court documents, and news articles
* NLP: Analyze IP-related text using spaCy and Stanford CoreNLP
* Graph analysis: Visualize IP landscapes and litigation networks using NetworkX and Cytoscape
* Output: IP landscape analysis, litigation tracking, and competitor IP analysis (Free sources: IP databases, court documents, news articles)

**8. Innovation Tracking: "Innovation Insight"**
* Web scraping: Extract innovation data from startup databases, news articles, and social media
* NLP: Analyze innovation-related text using NLTK and Gensim
* Graph analysis: Visualize innovation trends and startup networks using Gephi and Sigma.js
* Output: Innovation trend analysis, startup profiling, and industry mapping (Free sources: startup databases, news articles, social media)

**9. Competitor Analysis: "Competitor Canvas"**
* Web scraping: Extract competitor data from company websites, news articles, and social media
* NLP: Analyze competitor-related text using spaCy and TextBlob
* Graph analysis: Visualize competitor relationships and market positioning using NetworkX and Plotly
* Output: Competitor profiling, market positioning analysis, and competitor alert system (Free sources: company websites, news articles, social media)

**10. Technology Trend Analysis: "TechTrend"**
* Web scraping: Extract technology data from tech news websites, research papers, and patent databases
* NLP: Analyze technology-related text using TfidfVectorizer and scikit-learn
* Graph analysis: Visualize technology trends and innovation networks using Gephi and Cytoscape
* Output: Technology trend analysis, innovation forecasting, and competitor technology analysis (Free sources: tech news websites, research papers, patent databases)

These tools can be built using a combination of free and open-source libraries and tools, such as:

* Web scraping: Scrapy, BeautifulSoup, Selenium
* NLP: spaCy, NLTK, Stanford CoreNLP, Gensim, scikit-learn
* Graph analysis: NetworkX, Gephi, Cytoscape, Matplotlib, Plotly, Sigma.js

By leveraging these tools and techniques, you can gather valuable intelligence without relying on expensive commercial sources.
