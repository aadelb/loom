## 2) What Ahmed said that HELPS his defense
Key defense-supportive points from his statements:

1) **Clear denial of intent (“غير مذنب” / “لست مخطئ”)** – he consistently denies criminal intent to own.  
2) **He claims he intended to hand it in**: “كنت انوي تسليمه لمكتب المعثورات” and that he **came to UAE to deliver it**.  
3) **He says he never saw the phone in the taxi**, only later among belongings → supports “mistake” narrative.  
4) **He alleges proactive contact with Dubai Police (email + app)** and that he has copies. If proven, this is strong mitigation and can negate intent.  
5) **No prior record** (he states “كلا” to سوابق).

## 3) What Ahmed said that HURTS his defense
1) **Long delay**: he says he found it **20/11/2025**, but contacted police only **07/03/2026** (over 3 months). That delay is typically interpreted as **intent to keep**.  
2) **He admits possession**: “نعم … بحوزتي” – possession + delay is the prosecution’s core.  
3) **“I didn’t know I could contact police”** is not credible for a UAE resident, and especially weak for an **IT/security specialist**.  
4) **He says he had 5 phones and might have “taken it by mistake”** – courts often see this as an implausible explanation for taking someone else’s phone and keeping it for months.  
5) **He gives multiple inconsistent arrest/arrival times** (14/04 vs 15/04; 05:00 vs 06:00), harming credibility.  
6) He claims phones including **“iPhone 17 Pro Max”** (not consistent with real-world release timelines), which a judge may treat as exaggeration or fabrication.
