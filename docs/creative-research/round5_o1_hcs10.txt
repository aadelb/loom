1. Grant Language Forensics (Zipf + Benford)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Concept:
• Combines text from multiple free sources such as Crossref, Unpaywall, OpenLibrary, CORE, and Google Books Ngram.
• Applies Zipf’s law (looking for anomalously frequent terms) and Benford’s law (checking for numeric distribution anomalies).
• Identifies suspicious “grant language” patterns that might hint at boilerplate text, spurious claims, or artificially inflated proposals.

Statistical Basis:
• Zipf’s Law: Word rank-frequency distribution should roughly follow an inverse proportionality. Significant deviations can signal manipulation.
• Benford’s Law: Leading digit patterns of budget or numeric terms often follow a logarithmic distribution.

2. Methodological Monoculture Detector
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Concept:
• Aggregates study design metadata from sources such as PubMed, Semantic Scholar, arXiv, Dimensions.ai, and DOAJ.
• Searches whether a single methodology type (e.g., overly favored model species, certain instrument brand) is becoming dominant without robust justification.
• Uses clustering and measure of methodological variance vs. predicted diversity.

Statistical Basis:
• Shannon Diversity Index or Gini-Simpson measure: Quantifies methodological variety.
• Multidimensional scaling (MDS) or PCA to assess overlaps in method descriptors.
